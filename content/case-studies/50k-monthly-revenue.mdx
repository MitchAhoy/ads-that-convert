<CaseStudySection title="Context">
This account operates in ecommerce and needed paid acquisition to scale quickly without sacrificing unit economics.

The priority was profitable growth, not just top-line volume. That meant building a campaign structure that could separate high-intent demand from low-intent traffic from day one.
</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Strategy">
We built the account from scratch with product-segmented campaign architecture, strict search query controls, and a feed structure designed to improve relevance.

Budget was reallocated weekly toward products and campaigns with the strongest return profile, while underperforming segments were quickly trimmed.

As data quality improved, bidding strategy was progressively automated to scale while maintaining profitability thresholds.
</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Outcome">
Within the first two months, the account reached $50k/mth+ in Google Ads-attributed revenue.

Performance improved through tighter query quality, better product-level allocation, and iterative optimization loops that prioritized profitable conversion volume.
</CaseStudySection>
